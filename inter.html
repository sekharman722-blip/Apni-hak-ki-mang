Questions Every Citizen Should Ask

This page encourages citizens to stay informed, review public promises critically, and participate responsibly in democracy.

Public Issues & Political Promises

Employment & Youth

Many young citizens continue to raise concerns about unemployment, rising competition, and limited opportunities. Across governments and political parties, people often ask whether employment-related promises have been fulfilled effectively.

Education & Students

Students frequently discuss exam pressure, paper leaks, expensive education, and uneven access to quality institutions. Citizens expect long-term reforms instead of short-term political debates.

Farmers & Rural Areas

Farmers often speak about MSP, crop prices, water shortages, and financial pressure. Public discussion around agricultural reforms remains an important democratic issue.

Citizen Awareness

A strong democracy depends on informed citizens. Political parties across the spectrum make promises during elections related to jobs, development, infrastructure, education, healthcare, and public safety. Citizens have the right to review those promises carefully and ask questions respectfully.


Rather than supporting leaders blindly, people should compare policies, verify information from reliable sources, and evaluate how public decisions affect everyday life. Democracy becomes healthier when citizens participate responsibly and peacefully.


This website is intended as a public-awareness style design project encouraging civic discussion, youth participation, and democratic engagement. Readers should independently verify political claims and rely on trusted news and official information before forming conclusions.
